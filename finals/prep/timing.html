Prep · Timing rehearsal protocol

5 + 5 + 8 minutes. The protocol that makes it land.

Three timed windows decide the judging weekend: 5 minutes IP, 5 minutes RD, 8 minutes Final Share. Plus 2:30 robot matches × 3. This page describes how we rehearse, who runs the clock, and what the recovery rules are when we're going long.

Rehearsal protocol.

Daily for the last 14 days · twice daily for the last 7
Round 1 · cold reading

Read the script aloud, no script in hand, no clock.

  1. Each kid reads their line from memory, in order.
  2. If a kid forgets a line, we don't prompt — they recover in their own words.
  3. No timing pressure yet. Just the cadence.
Round 2 · timed run, no audience

Same script, with the clock running, no audience.

  1. Phone or watch on the table, face up, big numbers.
  2. Designated "clock owner" doesn't speak — only watches the time.
  3. Hit target finish (4:30 / 4:30 / 7:30) — not the hard limit.
  4. If we go long: stop the run, identify the slowdown, restart from the previous slide.
Round 3 · with adversarial Q&A

Same script, with a parent / mentor playing hostile judge.

  1. Adult plays a sceptical, fast-talking judge — interrupts, asks curveballs.
  2. Kids stay in character and stay on time.
  3. This trains the recovery muscles. The actual judges will be friendlier.
Round 4 · dress rehearsal

Full kit, full robot, full audience, full clock.

  1. Wear the team kit we'll wear on the day.
  2. Set up the robot exactly as we will at the venue.
  3. Bring 2–3 unfamiliar adults to play judges.
  4. Record the run if anyone consents — review the tape.

Recovery rules — when we're going long.

R1
If we're 30 seconds past target at the halfway slide:

Skip one specific named slide later in the deck. Pre-decided. Each deck has a designated "skip if needed" slide marked in the speaker notes.

R2
If we hit 4:00 / 4:00 / 7:00 and aren't at the closing slide:

Jump to the closing slide immediately. The closing line is non-negotiable. Better to land hard and short than to be cut off mid-sentence.

R3
If a Q&A answer is going long:

Whoever isn't speaking holds up two fingers — the speaker wraps in one sentence. We've practised this hand signal.

R4
If something genuinely breaks (robot, slide, voice):

Acknowledge it once, in plain English, then move on. Don't apologise repeatedly. Judges score the recovery, not the failure.

The clock owner role.

One named adult per session · same person across all sessions

Job description.

  1. Sits at the back of the room with the clock running on a phone, face up.
  2. Holds up fingers at 2 minutes remaining, 1 minute, 30 seconds, 10 seconds.
  3. Does not speak.
  4. After the session: writes down what the team did with the time. Feeds it back the same evening.
