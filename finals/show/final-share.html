8-min Final Share · the killer slot
01Final Share
Final Share & Feedback · 8 min

Where most teams collapse. Where we don't.

8 minutes to cement Core Values and Gracious Professionalism in the judges' last impression. Fully scripted. Fully rehearsed. Weaponised.

02Open · 30 sec
Beat 1 · open big

"Thank you. Before we share, we want to give something back."

Open by acknowledging the judges' time and naming one specific thing about this competition that's mattered to us. Not flattery — observation. (E.g. a volunteer who was kind, a moment we saw between two other teams, a thing the venue did well.)

Why: Sets a generous tone. Models the Gracious Professionalism we'll be scored on.
03Beat 2 · a win
Beat 2 · 1 min

One thing we did well — by name.

[ Pick a specific concrete win from this season. Not "we worked hard". A specific moment. Name the kid who made it happen. ]

Why: Specificity = credibility. Generic wins get scored as generic.
04Beat 3 · a loss
Beat 3 · 1 min

One thing we got wrong.

[ A specific failure from this season or this weekend. Not sanitised. What we tried, what didn't work, what we'd do differently. ]

Why: Honesty is observable Core Values. Teams that admit failure score above teams that don't.
05Beat 4 · learning from peers
Beat 4 · 1 min

One thing we learned from another team — by name.

[ Name another team. Name what they did. Name what we'd take away. This must be true — judges talk to each other. ]

Why: This is the slide that makes a Gracious Professionalism judge write down our team name. Almost no team does this.
06Beat 5 · learning from a volunteer
Beat 5 · 45 sec

A judge or volunteer told us something that stuck.

[ A specific thing a judge or volunteer said in IP/RD or in the pit that we'll carry forward. Name them if appropriate. ]

Why: Demonstrates we listened. Listening is the rarest observable quality in a 10-year-old presentation.
07Beat 6 · forward
Beat 6 · 1 min

What we're going to do about it.

  • 01
    [ Specific action ][ When. Who's responsible. ]
  • 02
    [ Specific action ][ When. Who's responsible. ]
  • 03
    [ Specific action ][ When. Who's responsible. ]
Why: Concrete next steps signal that the project is alive — not a school assignment that ends today.
08Beat 7 · ask back
Beat 7 · 1 min

One thing we'd love to ask you.

[ A genuine question to the judging panel. Something we don't know the answer to. Something they could helpfully comment on. ]

Why: Inverts the dynamic. Almost no team does this. Generates the most quotable judge feedback of the weekend.
09Beat 8 · thank by name
Beat 8 · 30 sec

Thank — by name.

Name two specific teams that were generous to us this weekend. Name one specific volunteer. Don't read off a list — say each name like we mean it.

Why: Public credit to peers is the most under-used Core Values lever in the room.
10Beat 9 · share the moment
Beat 9 · 30 sec

Our favourite moment from this weekend — before we leave the room.

[ One sentence each from two or three kids. Personal, specific, brief. ]

Why: Humanises the team in the judges' last impression. It's what they'll remember.
11Close
Beat 10 · close

Thank you for the 8 minutes.

No CTA. No pitch. Just thanks. Then silence. Then the kids stand up together.

Speaker note: The walk out matters as much as the walk in. Smile. Eye contact. No phones until you're outside the room.
