Strategic frame.

3 things change vs Harrogate

The Innovation Project is the crown jewel. The current Harrogate script reads like a project report. This rebuild turns it into a story with a contrarian thesis that judges remember 30 minutes later.

Change 01

Contrarian frame.

"We stopped inventing. We started listening." Most teams brag about what they invented. We lead with what we chose not to invent. Earns judge attention in the first 20 seconds.

Change 02

Technical depth visible.

RAG, AGUI, 4-part prompt engineering (Persona, Scope, Guardrails, Chain of Thought). Differentiates us from every team that says "we used ChatGPT."

Change 03

The Aaliya blueprint reveal.

Parallel structure to her Robot Design winning stroke. Both reveals follow the same pattern: "Our X isn't our best Y. Our Z is." Aaliya becomes the team's signature voice.

What we cut

The Duncan/Ri skit is cut. Skits are risky and most teams overact them. Replaced with a clean before/after sentence in Arvin's section.

Lower performance risk, sharper landing.

The bar

If this is delivered cleanly, Innovation Project rank should be top 3.

Combined with Robot Design (top 3–5), Core Values weave, and championship-grade Robot Performance — the Champion's Score moves into striking distance of 1st or 2nd in South Korea.

The full script.

Stage · Spoken line · Rubric hits

The spoken line is the contract. Stage direction is the choreography. Rubric hits at the bottom of each scene tell us why it's there.

Scene 01 Opening · the listening thesis
Esha 25 sec 50 words

Flip every other team's opener in 3 sentences.

Spoken
Annyeong-haseyo. We are Lord of the Bricks. Two-time UK regional champions. Last season, our innovation reached 3,000 people. We were proud. This season, we tried something different. We stopped inventing solutions. We started listening for problems. That changed everything.

Rubric hits

  • Sets up IP CREATE (innovation) by inverting the typical claim.
  • Plants Year-over-year arc with the 3,000-people anchor from last season.
  • Korean greeting signals respect without forcing it.

Why this opens differently

The "3,000 people last year" anchors credibility with a number, not an adjective. The contrarian thesis flips every other team's opener. Judges sit forward.

Scene 02 Research approach
Anaisa 25 sec 50 words

9 presentations. 8 hours. Then the meeting.

Spoken
Six of us had three FLL seasons behind us. Three of us were brand new. None of us knew archaeology. So we did 9 individual research presentations. Each of us picked one of the 5 innovation prompts and taught the rest of the team. Then 8 hours with Ken, a retired archaeologist. By the time we met the Kent Archaeological Society, we could speak their language.

Rubric hits

  • IP IDENTIFY (dual-marked Core Values · Discovery) — research from a variety of sources. 9 presentations + Ken + KAS. Exceeds candidate
  • IP DESIGN (dual-marked Core Values · Teamwork) — development process involved all team members. New members named, structurally framed.

Why this beats Harrogate

The old version used "brains about to pop like popcorn." Cut. New version surfaces the structural commitment: 9 presentations, 8 hours, then the meeting. Numbers > metaphors.

Scene 03 8 ideas, 1 problem
Guhan 30 sec 60 words

Name all 8. Show the listening in action.

Spoken
We came to KAS with 8 ideas. Soil colour sensors. Funding portals. LiDAR mapping. Pottery reconstruction tools. AI for storage. Annotation tools. Bone identification. Soil stratigraphy analysis. We pitched them all. They picked none. Instead, Jacob, Craig and Andy showed us 10,000 pages of unsearchable archives, and an email inbox they couldn't keep up with. That's when we stopped pitching, and started building.

Rubric hits

  • IP IDENTIFY — clearly defined problem.
  • Decision quality made visible through what was rejected.

Why this beats Harrogate

Old version mentions "8 ideas" abstractly. New version names all 8. This proves serious exploration. The "they picked none" line is the listening thesis in action. Hits hard.

Scene 04 The Information Gap
Arvin 25 sec 50 words

Before us / after us — in one parallel sentence.

Spoken
Before us, a researcher looking for the Ringlemere Cup would dig through 10,000 pages of journals, maps, blogs, and 150 years of newsletters. After us, they ask one question. They have the answer in seconds. 150 years of research. 10,000 webpages. 10,000 visitors a month. All available. All unusable. That's the Information Gap.

Rubric hits

  • IP IDENTIFY — clearly defined problem with quantification.
  • Sets up the Innovation reveal in slot 5.

Why this beats Harrogate

The Duncan/Ri skit is cut. The same before/after energy is delivered in 5 seconds through one parallel sentence. Lower performance risk, sharper landing.

Scene 05 Solution unveiled + live demo
Sakthi 25 sec 50 words

The reveal. The live moment. The invitation.

Holds up tablet, shows AI live on KAS website.

Spoken
So we built the Multi-Persona AI Assistant. One AI. Three personas. Ten thousand pages, searched in seconds. (holds up tablet, shows AI live on KAS website) Right now, this is running on the Kent Archaeological Society website. Anyone in Korea can type kentarchaeology.org and use it tonight.

Rubric hits

  • IP CREATE — detailed model that represents the solution. Live deployment beats every prototype.
  • Live demo grounds everything that follows.

Korea-specific

The "anyone in Korea can use it tonight" line invites the judges to verify the team's claim themselves. Confidence + transparency.

Scene 06 Three personas · 3 voices in 25 sec
Toby Elliot Esha #2 25 sec · ~8 each

Three voices. Three audiences. One AI.

Toby → Elliot → Esha. Each makes eye contact, delivers, then looks to the next speaker. Drilled 50 times.

Spoken · 3 voices, sharp handoffs
Toby

For an archaeologist, the AI gives excavation reports, citations, structured statistics. Professional grade. Like a research partner.

Elliot

For the public, it tells local stories. Roman roads, Saxon burials, medieval markets. Like a Kent museum in your pocket.

Esha

For students, it plays. Quizzes, simple language. We built two games in G-Develop so children can learn through play.

Rubric hits

  • IP CREATE (dual-marked Core Values · Innovation) — detailed explanation of innovation in solution. 3 differentiated personas.
  • Multiple voices = visible team contribution.
  • Each persona gets one memorable line. Judges see the breadth of the persona engineering.
Scene 07 Technical innovation · RAG · AGUI · Prompt Engineering
Flossie 40 sec 80 words

The section that earns the Exceeds comment.

Hands prompt engineering printout to judges.

Spoken
We don't just use AI. We engineered it. Three innovations. First: RAG. Retrieval Augmented Generation. The AI fetches answers only from KAS data. Never from the open internet. Trustworthy by design. Second: AGUI. Advanced Graphical User Interface. Eliminates blank-screen waits. Users see real-time loading, not freezes. Third: 4-part prompt engineering. Persona. Scope. Guardrails. Chain of Thought. Every answer is structured, on-topic, and source-cited. (hands prompt engineering printout to judges) That's what separates our AI from a chatbot.

Rubric hits

  • IP CREATE (dual-marked Core Values · Innovation) — Exceeds candidate
  • IP CREATE — clear explanation of innovative attachments → 3 named innovations.
  • This is the section that earns the comment box on the Exceeds column.

Why this section is critical

Lifts Innovation Project rank from "good team that used AI" to "team that engineered AI." Every judge will know teams that say "we used ChatGPT." Lord of the Bricks engineered the architecture.

Scene 08 Live deployment + expert validation
Elliot #2 30 sec 60 words

Real-world impact, named recipients, direct quotes.

Hands KAS newsletter printout to judges. Then iteration list.

Spoken
This isn't a prototype. It's live on the Kent Archaeological Society website, serving 10,000 visitors a month. (hands KAS newsletter printout to judges) The Society featured us in their official newsletter. Their Digital Manager, Jacob Scott, called our work 'mind-boggling.' Their Curator, Andy Ward, called it inspiring. Ken, the retired archaeologist, wrote: 'no mere computer game, but a serious piece of work.' (hands iteration list to judges) We made 30+ improvements based on KAS feedback before launch.

Rubric hits

  • IP ITERATE — clear evidence of improvements based on feedback. 30+ improvements.
  • IP ITERATE — solution shared with multiple people/groups. KAS team, expert review, newsletter audience.
  • IP COMMUNICATE (dual-marked Core Values · Impact) — real-world validated impact with named recipients and quotes. Exceeds candidate
Scene 09 The blueprint reveal + closer
Aaliya 35 sec 70 words Winning stroke

"Our AI isn't our best innovation. Our blueprint is."

Aaliya holds up blueprint one-pager. After the chant, all 9 hold position with eye contact.

Spoken · the line judges write down
You've heard about our AI assistant. Our AI isn't our best innovation. Our blueprint is. (holds up blueprint one-pager) Any historical society in the world can take what we built and adapt it for their own city. Three free pilots are already lined up. We didn't just digitise Kent's history. We built the system that can digitise any city's history. That's our innovation. Thank you for hearing our story. Gamsahamnida. [All 9 together, eye contact with judges]: WE ARE LORD OF THE BRICKS!
The Aaliya pattern

Same speaker, same reframe structure as her Robot Design winning stroke: "Our X isn't our best Y. Our Z is." In Robot Design — robot vs team. In Innovation — AI vs blueprint. Two presentations, one signature voice. Judges remember Aaliya as the team's reveal artist.

Why this is the winning stroke

  1. Parallel pattern to Robot Design. Two reveals, one signature voice. The pattern is what wins.
  2. Reframes the entire presentation. Before this line: "great team, built an AI for Kent." After this line: "great team, built a replicable system for civilisation." The category of impact changes mid-sentence.
  3. Scalability is the rare innovation move. Most teams build a thing. Lord of the Bricks built a thing that builds things. Meta-engineering — almost never seen at this age group.
  4. Three free pilots = proof not promise. Not "we hope to scale." Already scaling.
  5. Korean gratitude + identity closer. "Gamsahamnida" reciprocates Esha's Annyeong-haseyo opening. The all-together close lands the team identity and the dual-marked Fun/Pride row.

Rubric hits

  • IP CREATE (dual-marked Core Values · Innovation) — Exceeds
  • IP COMMUNICATE (dual-marked Core Values · Impact) — scaling to other societies. Exceeds
  • IP COMMUNICATE (dual-marked Core Values · Fun) — pride/enthusiasm visible across all 9 in closer. Exceeds candidate
  • IP DESIGN (dual-marked Core Values · Teamwork) — visible "all 9" finale.

Why this script blows judges off.

5 mechanics most teams don't combine
M1

The contrarian opener.

"We stopped inventing. We started listening." Every other team's opener is a problem statement followed by their solution. We lead with a philosophical inversion. Judges remember this 30 minutes later.

M2

Named rejected ideas.

8 ideas listed in Guhan's section. Most teams say "we considered many ideas." We name 8. Specificity is what makes decision quality credible.

M3

Technical depth as differentiator.

RAG, AGUI, 4-part prompt engineering. Every other team mentions ChatGPT or Claude. We explain the architecture. AI-literate judges notice immediately. Others are impressed by engineering vocabulary handled confidently by children.

M4

Live deployment over claims.

"Right now, this is running. Anyone in Korea can type kentarchaeology.org and use it tonight." Most teams show screenshots. We invite verification.

M5

The Aaliya meta-reframe at the close.

The blueprint reveal at the very end reframes everything the judges just heard. Combined with her Robot Design reveal, Aaliya becomes the signature voice. That's a presentation pattern that wins.

Rehearsal protocol.

6 weeks · special focus on the personas section

Memorise structure and key lines. Exact wording will move slightly between rehearsals — that's fine. Structure must not.

Week 01 Each speaker memorises their own section. Hit time targets within 3 sec.
Week 02 Run the full script as a team. Practice handoffs and the 3-voice personas section (Toby → Elliot → Esha). Time the full thing.
Week 03 Drill Aaliya's blueprint reveal in isolation 30 times. The line that anchors the entire impression. Pace, eye contact, pause before "Our AI isn't our best innovation."
Week 04 Full run with artefact movements: tablet demo, printout handoffs, blueprint reveal. These transitions are where teams stumble.
Week 05 Full run in front of external audience. Get feedback. Adjust.
Week 06 Polish only. No structural changes. Two full runs per day. Last week before Korea.

Special focus · Slot 6 — the personas

Three speakers in 25 seconds requires sharp handoffs.

Each speaker should make eye contact with the judges, deliver, then look to the next speaker. Practice this 50 times.
