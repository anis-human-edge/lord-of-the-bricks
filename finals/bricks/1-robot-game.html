Strategic frame.

110-pt gap = match execution problem, not robot problem

UK Robot Performance already won. The 110-pt gap between 545 (theoretical) and 435 (Regionals actual) is not a robot problem. It is a match execution problem.

Specific failure modes — what costs points under pressure

Diagnosed
  • A run fails mid-execution and the team loses 60+ seconds deciding what to do
  • A "90%" run hits its 10% on the day that matters
  • Battery or temperature shifts behaviour in subtle ways
  • Korean mat texture or paint differs from UK mat
  • Team makes timing errors under pressure (resets too slow, panics, restarts unnecessarily)

What this playbook does

The fix
  • Tighter table-side roles — Operator / Resetter / Spotter / Time-caller
  • Failure decision tree the Operator can execute in 3 seconds
  • Pre-match routine that locks focus from T−30 to T−0
  • Between-match discipline that resists "one more change"
  • Mental preparation that converts capability into outcome

Pre-match routine.

T−30 → T−0 · countdown discipline
T−30 min

Equipment check.

  • Robot charged to 100%, last drained < 4 hours ago
  • All 8 attachments in starting positions
  • Aligners and jigs in carry bag
  • Custom menu screen loads correctly
  • Backup batteries charged
  • Spare bricks (most failure-prone pieces) packed
T−20 min

Mental calibration.

  • Whole team huddle. 2 minutes max.
  • Operator states: "Run order today is [X]. Failure protocol: skip when in doubt."
  • Everyone confirms.
  • L! O! T! B! Lord of the Bricks chant.
T−15 min

Approach to table area.

  • Walk together. Slowly. No rushing.
  • Avoid bumping into other teams' tables.
  • Greet referees by name (read name tag).
T−10 min

Practice table — if available.

  • One quick alignment test.
  • Test the gyro on a straight-line run.
  • Do NOT run full sequences. You'll waste battery and burn timing memory.
T−3 min

At the competition table.

  • Operator and Resetter at the table.
  • Spotter and Time-caller flanking.
  • Other 5 members 6 feet back, silent.
  • Operator places robot in launch position.
  • Eyes up. Breath in. Breath out.
T−0

Match starts.

  • Referee says go.
  • Operator: "Go."

Between-match routine.

First 5 min · 15-60 min · what to fix

First 5 minutesImmediately after match.

  • All 9 huddle. No phones, no parents.
  • One round: each person says one observation. Just observations, no judgments.
  • Operator (or coach) decides: one change for the next match. One only. Not two, not three.

15 to 60 minPit work + recovery.

  • Robot back to pit. Charge if needed (rarely needed for a 2:30 run).
  • Inspect attachments for damage. Most common failure: pin loosening on Run [TBD].
  • Hydrate. Eat something light (banana, biscuit). Not sugary drinks.
  • Use the bathroom in plenty of time before the next call.

What to fixYes — these only.

  • Mechanical: yes (loose pin, slipped rubber band).
  • Code: only if a specific bug was clearly visible, AND the fix is verified-safe.
  • Strategy: no. Don't change run order mid-event.

What NOT to fixThe temptations to resist.

  • "We could try X instead" → no. Stick to the plan.
  • "Let me just rewrite this line" → no. Untested code on match day is catastrophic.
  • "What if we add a sensor" → no. You go with what got you here.

Korea-specific variables.

6 things that don't exist at UK Nationals
Variable 01

Mat differences.

Friction, age, paint imperfections may differ. Plan — arrive early on practice day, run all 8 runs once, note distance-per-rotation differences, recalibrate if needed.

Variable 02

Battery + temperature.

Motor behaviour shifts with venue temperature. Plan — at least one full-match simulation in the venue temperature before competing.

Variable 03

Time zone + sleep.

Korea is 9 hours ahead of UK. Jet lag is real. Plan — land at least 2 days before. Sleep schedule adjusted by 2 hours per day starting 5 days before departure.

Variable 04

Foreign noise environment.

Louder venue, more languages, more energy. Robot is unaffected — team focus may drop. Plan — practice runs at home with loud music or chatter. Build noise immunity.

Variable 05

Referee variation.

Touch penalties + line-side rulings may be interpreted differently. Plan — at registration, the coach asks 2 specific edge-case questions. Get written confirmation if possible.

Variable 06

Equipment damage in transit.

The robot may shift in transit. Plan — rebuild any loose connection on arrival. Test all 8 runs on Day 1. Do not assume the robot survived the flight intact.

Mental preparation.

The 110-pt gap is mostly mental

Train these habits in the next weeks. They're how capability becomes outcome under pressure.

Habit 01

The 3-second rule.

When something goes wrong, the team has 3 seconds to decide and act. Train by simulating failures during practice — knock a piece, mis-align — see how fast the team adjusts.

Habit 02

The "no replay" rule.

After a run, no replaying what happened. Observe, decide one fix, move on. End every practice with the same protocol: huddle, one observation each, one fix decided.

Habit 03

The L-O-T-B reset.

When tension builds, anyone says "L-O-T-B" quietly. The rest respond "Lord of the Bricks." Resets focus. Builds team rhythm under stress.

Habit 04

The breath.

Operator takes 3 slow breaths before pressing start. Every match, every time. This is not optional.

Habit 05

Win or lose, chant together.

Regardless of outcome, after the final match the team does the full closing chant together. Builds identity that survives results.

Score targeting framework.

The strategic insight — match-by-match, not match-perfect
Floor 435 What you hit at Regionals. The non-negotiable baseline.
Stretch 480 What a clean Match 1 should deliver.
Strong 510 What a clean Match 2 with one retry should deliver.
Ceiling 545 A perfect Match 3. This is the goal.

The strategic insight.

You do not need to hit 545. You need to maximise the highest of 3 matches. That means the goal in each match is different:

  • Match 1 — clear the floor (435+)
  • Match 2 — lift to strong (480+)
  • Match 3 — swing for ceiling (520+)

A score of 510 across all 3 matches is better than one 545 followed by two 380s.

Consistency wins.
