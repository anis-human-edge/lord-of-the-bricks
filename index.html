02 / Why Mexico
The Headline

Why Mexico Matters.

The FIRST LEGO League International Open Championship is the world stage for school robotics. 90 teams. 30+ countries. Four days in Guadalajara, Jalisco.

We earned our spot. Kent Regional Champions, twice. UK National Knockout Champions. Second in the UK on Robot Performance.

We are one of a small handful of UK teams competing. We will be wearing the Union Jack on our shirts. The team that wins is judged on three things: a robot, an innovation project, and core values. We have built all three from scratch.

This is what we are flying to defend.

04 / The Robot
Built from mistakes

World Dominator.
Version 4.

Built from three years of mistakes.

This is our fourth robot. Each one taught us something. World Dominator runs on Pybricks instead of LEGO block code — closer to how professional engineers actually work.

We set ourselves an ambitious target this season: 545 points. We combined 15 missions into 8 efficient runs. Modular attachments drop in and lift off without pins. Custom gearboxes balance speed and torque. A gyro sensor keeps the robot driving straight on long runs. A custom menu auto-loads the next mission with a single touch.

We tested every run more than 200 times. Our goal is 99% consistency. After Nationals, we made over 50 design and code changes based on failure analysis.

05 / The Innovation
Live in production

A Multi-Persona AI Assistant.
Live. In Production.

We didn't build a prototype. We built and deployed real software for a real client.

The Kent Archaeological Society holds 150 years of records. Over 10,000 pages of journals, reports, maps, and artefacts. Archaeologists, students, and the public bombard them with emails because the data is impossible to search.

We partnered with their experts — Jacob, Craig, and Andy — and built them a Multi-Persona AI Assistant that searches every page in seconds.

What makes it innovative: three personas built into the same tool.

01

TobyThe Archaeologist

Speaks in technical terms. Returns excavation data, statistics, citations.

02

ElliotThe Public

Translates technical archives into local stories and historical facts.

03

AliyaThe Student

Makes Kent's history simple, fun, engaging. Includes a Surprise Button.

How it's built

Prompt engineering and RAG, end to end.

We built the prototype in Claude. Used Replit for the production build. Implemented prompt engineering with four layers — Persona, Scope, Guardrails, and Chain of Thought. Added a RAG system to make sure the AI only pulls from KAS-owned content. Added an AGUI module to fix slow response times.

It is officially live on the Kent Archaeological Society website. It has been featured in their newsletter.

We then went one step further. We built a Generic Source Code blueprint so any historical society in the world can deploy the same system on their own archives.

06 / Beyond
Track record

This Isn't Our
First Real-World Build.

Last season we tackled microplastics. We reached 3,000+ people, wrote books, and presented at Parliament.

Before Unearthed, we worked on microplastics. We built educational games, ran school sessions, and reached over 3,000 people across the UK. We were invited to the UK Houses of Parliament. We presented at the Croydon AI Summit. We were honoured by the Mayor of Croydon.

That season taught us a hard lesson: building a great tool is not enough. You need a real client to adopt it. So this year we did it differently. We found the partner first. Then we built the solution.

This is why our AI is on a live website today, not sitting on our laptops.
