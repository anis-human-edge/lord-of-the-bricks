01 / The story in 100 words
The short version

The story
in 100 words

Lord of the Bricks is a FIRST LEGO League team from Croydon. Eight children. Six schools. No academy, no sponsor-built lab, no professional coaches. Two parents, a doctor and an IT architect, ran every session. In the 2025–26 Unearthed season the team became Kent Regional Champions, UK Knockout Challenge Champions, and took 2nd place in Robot Performance at both the UK Nationals and the FIRST LEGO League Korea Open in Jeonju, South Korea, representing Great Britain. Their score in Korea: 540 out of a possible 545. Along the way they built a working AI assistant for the Kent Archaeological Society. It is live today.

07 / The Innovation Project
The Innovation Project

While the robot trained,
they built an AI.

While the robot trained for Korea, the same eight built something else: a live AI assistant for the Kent Archaeological Society. 150 years of journals and excavation records. 10,000 pages, searchable by almost no one. The assistant answers in three voices: one for archaeologists, one for students, one for the public. Prompt-engineered by the children, tested with the Society’s own curators, and open-sourced so any historical society can copy it.

08 / The lesson

The lesson

The lesson

Two silvers. The gap between 500 and 540 was 200 hours of work. The gap between 540 and 545 is next season’s fuel. Resilience never came from a poster or an assembly. It came from a scoreboard, a long flight home, and a decision to go back to the table.
