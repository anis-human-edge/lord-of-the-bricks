## Strategic frame

The Innovation Project is your crown jewel. The current Harrogate script reads like a project report. This rebuild turns it into a story with a contrarian thesis that judges will remember.

Three things change versus Harrogate:

1. **Contrarian frame.** "We stopped inventing. We started listening." Most teams brag about what they invented. You lead with what you chose NOT to invent. That's the line that earns judge attention in the first 20 seconds.

2. **Technical depth visible.** RAG, AGUI, 4-part prompt engineering (Persona, Scope, Guardrails, Chain of Thought). These differentiate you from every team that says "we used ChatGPT."

3. **The Aaliya blueprint reveal.** Parallel structure to her Robot Design winning stroke. Both reframes follow the same pattern: *"Our X isn't our best Y. Our Z is."* This makes Aaliya the team's signature voice. Judges remember the pattern.

The Harrogate Duncan/Ri skit is cut. Skits are risky and most teams overact them. Replaced with a clean before/after sentence in Arvin's section.

## The full script

### 1. ESHA — Opening: the listening thesis (25 sec)

> "Annyeong-haseyo. We are Lord of the Bricks. Two-time UK regional champions.
>
> Last season, our innovation reached 3,000 people. We were proud.
>
> This season, we tried something different. **We stopped inventing solutions. We started listening for problems.**
>
> That changed everything."

**Why this opens differently:**
Korean greeting signals respect. The "3,000 people last year" anchors credibility with a number, not an adjective. The contrarian thesis ("we stopped inventing") flips every other team's opener. Judges sit forward.

**Rubric hits:** Sets up IP CREATE (innovation) by inverting the typical claim. Plants Year-over-year arc.

---

### 2. ANAISA — Research approach (25 sec)

> "Six of us had three FLL seasons behind us. Three of us were brand new. None of us knew archaeology.
>
> So we did **9 individual research presentations.** Each of us picked one of the 5 innovation prompts and taught the rest of the team.
>
> Then **8 hours with Ken**, a retired archaeologist.
>
> By the time we met the Kent Archaeological Society, we could speak their language."

**Rubric hits:**
- IP IDENTIFY (dual-marked, Core Values Discovery): "Research from a variety of sources" → 9 presentations + Ken + KAS = Exceeds candidate
- IP DESIGN (dual-marked, Core Values Teamwork): "Development process involved all team members" → new members named, structurally framed

**Why this beats Harrogate's version:**
Old version used "brains about to pop like popcorn." Cut. New version surfaces the structural commitment: 9 presentations, 8 hours, then the meeting. Numbers > metaphors.

**Why this beats Harrogate:**
Old version mentions "8 ideas" abstractly. New version NAMES all 8. This proves serious exploration. The "they picked none" line is the listening thesis in action. Hits hard.

**Rubric hits:**
- IP IDENTIFY: clearly defined problem
- Decision quality made visible through what was rejected

## Why this script blows judges off

Five mechanics most teams don't combine:

**1. The contrarian opener.**
"We stopped inventing. We started listening." Every other team's opener will be a problem statement followed by their solution. You lead with a philosophical inversion. Judges remember this 30 minutes later.

**2. Named rejected ideas.**
8 ideas listed in Guhan's section. Most teams say "we considered many ideas." You name 8. Specificity is what makes decision quality credible.

**3. Technical depth as differentiator.**
RAG, AGUI, 4-part prompt engineering. Every other team mentions ChatGPT or Claude as tools. You explain the architecture. The judges who understand AI will notice immediately. The judges who don't will be impressed by the engineering vocabulary handled confidently by children.

**4. Live deployment over claims.**
"Right now, this is running. Anyone in Korea can type kentarchaeology.org and use it tonight." Most teams show screenshots. You invite verification.

**5. The Aaliya meta-reframe at the close.**
The blueprint reveal at the very end reframes everything the judges just heard. Combined with her Robot Design reveal, Aaliya becomes the signature voice of Lord of the Bricks. That's a presentation pattern that wins.

## Rehearsal protocol

**Week 1:** Each speaker memorises their own section. Hit time targets within 3 seconds.

**Week 2:** Run the full script as a team. Practice handoffs and the 3-voice personas section (Toby → Elliot → Esha). Time the full thing.

**Week 3:** Drill Aaliya's blueprint reveal in isolation 30 times. This is the line that anchors the entire impression. Pace, eye contact, pause before "Our AI isn't our best innovation."

**Week 4:** Full run with artefact movements: tablet demo, printout handoffs, blueprint reveal. These transitions are where teams stumble.

**Week 5:** Full run in front of external audience. Get feedback. Adjust.

**Week 6 (last week before Korea):** Polish only. Two full runs per day. No structural changes.

**Special focus:** the personas section in slot 6. Three speakers in 25 seconds requires sharp handoffs. Each speaker should make eye contact with the judges, deliver, then look to the next speaker. Practice this 50 times.
